# LinkedIn Post — Based on Research

```
Something is happening across multiple markets at once.

Bairong (Chinese, publicly traded) is selling AI agents on outcomes — 
they call it "Results-as-a-Service." Revenue up 31% last year.

Zendesk just started charging $1.50 per resolved ticket instead of 
per seat. No resolution, no payment.

A.Team is assembling entire product teams in 48 hours and billing 
on delivery. They deployed a squad for Sandow that built a data 
platform in 30 days.

Braintrust cut Nestle's staffing fees by $310K on a single project 
by dropping the markup from 35% to 10%.

Smart.coop in Europe has 120,000 freelancers paying 6.5% for a 
cooperative wrapper that gives them employment benefits.

The pattern: pricing is shifting from inputs to outcomes. From 
hours to results. From "what does it cost" to "what do I get."

The staffing industry charges 35-50% to match a resume to a req. 
That made sense when matching was hard. It doesn't make sense 
when an algorithm can do it in seconds.

The question isn't whether this shift happens. It's who builds 
the infrastructure for it.
```
