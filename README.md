# Guild Protocol

## A Peer-to-Peer System for Knowledge Work

The staffing industry extracts $184-189 billion annually from knowledge workers through information asymmetry, contractual traps, and layered intermediaries. When a client pays $100/hour for a contractor, the worker gets $50-65 after agencies, MSPs, and subcontractors each take their cut. The entire model depends on opacity: workers don't know their bill rate, clients don't know what workers receive, and everyone pays conversion fees and non-competes to keep it that way.

The Guild Protocol replaces this with infrastructure for direct, outcome-based knowledge work. Work is organized as quests with defined scope and escrowed budgets. Teams assemble through algorithmic matching optimized for delivery, not demographics. Reputation is earned through verified outcomes, portable across platforms, and owned by the worker. No recruiters. No markup arbitrage. No credential theater. Veterans lead and mentor. Apprentices learn by doing. Everyone rises on results.

This is not a marketplace. Marketplaces become the new middlemen. This is a protocol -- open infrastructure that any platform can implement, where the rules are transparent and the value flows to the people who create it.

## Why This Matters

The staffing industry's extraction is not a market inefficiency waiting to be optimized. It is the business model.

- **$184-189B extracted annually** by intermediaries inserting themselves between workers and employers, with 35-75% markups on contractor labor
- **53% of candidates face bait-and-switch tactics** where actual job duties, salary, or employment type differ from what was advertised (Greenhouse survey, ~3,000 workers)
- **Workers lose ~$39,000/year** compared to full-time equivalents after accounting for missing benefits, equity, bonuses, healthcare, and PTO -- while doing the same work
