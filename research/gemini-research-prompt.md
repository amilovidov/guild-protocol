# Gemini Deep Research Prompt: Knowledge Work Organization Shifts

I'm researching fundamental shifts in how knowledge work gets organized, priced, and matched. Not the future of remote work or gig economy think pieces — I want concrete examples of new models emerging now.

## STARTING POINTS I'VE IDENTIFIED:
- Bairong (Chinese public company) selling AI agents on outcomes, not seats — "Results-as-a-Service"
- Staffing industry ($650B globally) facing pressure as 35-50% markups become harder to justify
- LinkedIn declining (views down 50% YoY, 27% ghost jobs) creating opening for alternatives

I want to find examples across these categories:

## 1. OUTCOME-BASED PRICING
- Companies charging for results instead of hours/seats (software, services, consulting)
- Performance-based contracting models
- Success fees, gainsharing, revenue-share arrangements in professional services
- Failures: who tried this and why did it fail?

## 2. REPUTATION & CREDENTIALING
- Alternatives to resumes/credentials for proving capability
- Portable reputation systems (not locked to one platform)
- Skill verification that isn't just certifications
- On-chain or verifiable credentials for work history
- How gaming (esports, guilds, CTF competitions) handles reputation — any crossover to professional work?

## 3. MATCHING & TEAM ASSEMBLY
- Platforms doing algorithmic team assembly (not just individual freelancer matching)
- AI-powered matching for knowledge work
- Anything that looks like "guilds" forming — groups of independents coordinating for larger contracts
- Alternatives to staffing agencies gaining traction

## 4. EMPLOYMENT ALTERNATIVES
- Countries or regions where independent/contract work is structured differently than US
- Cooperatives, collectives, or new legal structures for knowledge workers
- Portable benefits models (not tied to single employer)
- How other countries handle the "gig economy" classification problem

## 5. AI-HUMAN HYBRID MODELS
- How are teams restructuring around AI capabilities?
- Small teams + AI outperforming large teams — any documented examples?
- New roles emerging at the human-AI boundary

## 6. SKEPTICISM & FAILURE MODES
- Why hasn't outcome-based work taken over already? What are the real barriers?
- Platforms or companies that tried these models and failed
- Critiques from labor economists or worker advocates

## 7. EMPLOYER/DEMAND SIDE

- How do Fortune 500 companies structure contingent workforce management? (MSPs, VMS systems)
- What are procurement departments' real criteria when selecting staffing vendors?

- Companies that have brought contingent workforce management in-house — how did it go?
- What would make an enterprise switch from traditional staffing to something new?
- How do enterprises measure staffing vendor performance? What metrics matter?
- Case studies of large consulting/services engagements priced on outcomes vs time & materials
